Greece

The trip of your dreams! If you're like me and Greece has been on your bucket list for forever, I have the best itinerary for you.

We started our trip off in Santorini. There are a lot of flights that go into that airport, and since it's at the bottom of the main islands, it's the perfect starting place. In Santorini we stayed in a charming Airbnb located somewhat near Fira. On our first night, we walked around nearby, and found a cute dinner place with authentic Greek food. As you can imagine, it was great!

The next day we had a sunset boat tour at 3 PM. We booked this through GetYourGuide, where you can find lots of different options depending on your preferences. Before that, we walked over to Fira, walked around the town which had a lot of different shops. Pro tip: leave a little space in your suitcase, you're going to want to shop! The town is very picturesque and exactly what you imagine when you think of Greece. We went on the boat tour where they took us around the island, we were able to jump off the boat and swim, and they provided lots of snacks and dinner. It was so beautiful going around the island, and the water was so blue! After we got dropped off, we walked around a little more before going to Kaliya. We had originally intended to just go for drinks, but the menu looked too good, we ended up getting some food as well. The restaurant was very girly and cute inside with lots of pink flowers around the entire place!

Next on the agenda, we were headed to Paros! However, our ferry wasn't until 3:45 PM so we had time to explore. The main thing when visiting Santorini is Oia. We taxi'd there, about a 20 minute drive from Fira, and walked around. We got lucky that our taxi driver offered to wait, and drive us to the ferry station after. Don't be afraid to ask, most of the time everyone is very willing to help! We walked around Oia, went to the main pictures spot, of course, and took lots of flicks. We had more time than we anticipated, so we were able to have a gorgeous lunch by the water. One thing to note, if you want to get good photos and not wait in line, try and get there as early as possible. We just missed the long lines that began to form after we left. The area in Oia is so cute and picturesque, there are so many stores you can walk in and out of while looking at all the beautiful artworks they have.

Next stop: Paros! Our hotel in Paros, Marioly Studios was the most charming place, owned by a mother and daughter. It doesn't get more Mamma Mia than that. They were both so accomodating and helpful with anything and everything we asked for. It was located in Parikia, one of the main towns in Paros, with lots of restaurants. On our first night, we walked from the hotel towards the main restaurants earlier, and ate at one of the first ones we found. There were many options and we ended up trying out a good amount of them by the end of our trip in Paros.

Our second day was a much needed chill beach day. We had gone in late April, so it was still off-season, meaning a lot of things weren't open yet. The owner of our hotel drove us out to Marcello Beach Club, supposed to be well known in Paros, but it was undergoing construction. However, she showed us another beach club, walking distance from the hotel that was perfect for us. The beach was secluded, the restaurant wasn't too packed, and the food was amazing! We spent most of our day on the beach, with towels we borrowed from the hotel, and going back and forth from the restaurant to get drinks. The water was crystal clear, and so refreshing! It was unreal to think that we were in the middle of Greece! After lounging for a while, we walked back into the main area of Parikia and walked around the different shops and sights in the town. It was quite secluded, but that added to the tranquility of our trip. For dinner, we walked to a place in between the beach and our hotel, which had seating right on the water. The sunset was gorgeous!

Our last day in Paros took place in Naousa. This town is very well-known in Paros, and is usually the tourist stop. We spent most of our day here walking around the picturesque streets, exploring random places, like this church we happened to stumble upon, and shopping. We first grabbed lunch at Fotis All Day Bar which had great food, perfect to get our energy up to start exploring. After walking around, seeing the different places, we went for our dinner reservations at Sigi Ikthios, which is very well-known in Paros. This place is properly known for its dance party during dinner, but since it was off season, we sadly didn't get to experience that. Paros is definitely a more underrated place in Greece, and not on the top of everyone's list. However, it has a unique charm that adds the Mamma Mia aspect into your Greek vacation!

Stop three: Mykonos! We took our ferry in the morning and got to Mykonos in the afternoon. The transportation in Mykonos is limited, so be prepared to walk with your suitcases. We checked into our Airbnb which was in the perfect spot! It had the cutest blue balcony right in a very picturesque street. We walked around the town and got lunch at Souvlaki Story. You will have the best gyros here! There are so many different options all looking amazing! We continued to walk around and shop, before going back to get ready!

We got dinner along the water, where the view was stunning! On our last day in Mykonos, we decided to go one of the beach clubs we found on a TikTok, lol. The sand on the beach was so so soft! We hung out there, the vibe was electric, we swam in the water, and enjoyed the atmosphere. If you go to Mykonos, you are sure to have a great time!

Last stop is Athens! If you're going to Athens, be prepared to walk a lot. We got there late at night, and ordered in...guilty. The next day we woke up early because we had a lot of sites to see. From our Airbnb, we walked over to the Ancient Agora of Athens. We got tickets at the door which were free! This will happen a lot especially on weekdays. After that we walked towards the Temple of Zeus, which was very easy to get in. Most of these sites you will be able to get tickets at the door. It was super cool to walk around these sites, an amazing experience. There was also a great view of the Acropolis on the hill. After that, we walked towards the Panathenaic Stadium, the oldest olympic stadium. This was so cool! We got to stand on the podium, run on the track, and sit in the throne at the end of the stadium. From here, we had time to kill until our dinner, so we walked to the national garden. On the way we stopped for some Greek frozen yogurt, delicious!

We went to dinner at a place called Dionysos Zonar's which had the perfect view of the Acropolis. We made our reservations for 7:30 PM, perfect for sunset. The food was amazing, definitely fine dining (only the best for our last night in Greece!). After dinner, we went back to the Airbnb and decided to go out. After submitting my last paper of the semester...we went to the Gazi area, notorious for its nightlife. There are lots of fun bars in the area, and it's very lively and fun. Everyone in Athens are very friendly and welcoming!

Greece is a dream come true! This itinerary is the perfect relaxing and adventure-filled trip with a mix of everything to make sure you don't get too burnt out. We went for 10 days, from April 9-19 (off-season, but still fun!).
